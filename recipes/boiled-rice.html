Boiled White Rice Recipe

Description

Boiled white rice is a staple of Asian cooking. Every region has their own way of preparing this dish. Here is one way of preparing boiled white rice. This can be eaten with a wide varity of meat or vegitable dishes, from curries to kababs.

Ingredients

  • Uncooked white Basmati long grain rice, 2 cups.
  • Salt, 1 teaspoon.
  • Butter, 2 teaspoons

Steps

  1. Soak the rice in cold water for about 30 minutes. Move the rice around drain the water and refill with fresh cold water. Do this 3 times.
  2. Fill a medium sauce pan 3/4 full of water and place on high heat.
  3. Once the water stats boiling, drain the soaking uncooked rice and pour into the boiling water. Add a teaspoon of salt.
  4. Let the rice boil for about 8 minutes. With an insulated spoon carefully remove a few grains of rice and taste. If the rice is a little chewey, like al-dente pasta, it is ready. It will get soft as it continues to cook in residual heat.
  5. Turn off the heat and carefully pour the contents of the saucepan into a sive to drain the hot water. This can also be done by using a lid on the pan.
  6. Return the drained rice back into the saucepan. Place it on a cool surface and add the butter to the rice.
  7. Cover and let it sit for about 10 minutes.
  8. Your delicious, fluffy, buttery, white rice is ready.
